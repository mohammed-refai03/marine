Critical Threats to Our Oceans

Human activity has pushed marine ecosystems to the brink. Understanding these major crises is the first critical step toward global restoration.

Plastic Pollution Crisis

Over 11 million metric tons of plastic enter our oceans annually. This non-biodegradable waste destroys marine habitats, enters the food chain, and fatally traps thousands of marine animals daily.

Read Threat Analysis

Coral Reef Destruction

Rising temperatures and destructive fishing practices trigger massive coral bleaching events. We have already lost half of our global coral reefs, threatening 25% of all ocean species.

Read Threat Analysis

Commercial Overfishing

Industrial fleets systematically deplete global fish stocks faster than populations can recover. Destructive practices like bottom trawling decimate entire sea floor habitats indiscriminately.

Read Threat Analysis

Accelerating Climate Change

Oceans absorb roughly 90% of atmospheric excess heat. This causes rapid acidification and extreme thermal fluctuations, altering critical currents and historical migration patterns.

Read Threat Analysis
Who We Are

Pioneering Scientific Restoration & Activism

Stackly is a global network of marine scientists, conservationists, and volunteers. We believe that saving our oceans requires a combination of real-time monitoring technology and direct local community action.

Through our custom ecosystem data networks and coral propagation programs, we are actively turning the tide against coral bleaching, plastic pollution, and overfishing in critical marine sectors globally.

Marine Species Under Our Watch

Every organism plays a vital role. By safeguarding these cornerstone species, we secure the structural integrity of the global marine food web.

Sea Turtles

Crucial for maintaining healthy seagrass beds and coral ecosystems, now threatened dramatically by plastic ingestion and nesting site degradation.

Oceanic Dolphins

As apex predators, dolphins serve as key indicators of broader ecosystem stability. They face persistent dangers from commercial gillnets.

Cornerstone Corals

Providing essential nursery structures and safety zones for over a quarter of all known marine life forms globally.

Great Whales

Whales play a massive role in global carbon capture systems and fertilizing phytoplankton layers across ocean currents.
